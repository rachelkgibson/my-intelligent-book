# Concept Taxonomy: Engineering Practicum IV Honors

11 categories covering all 85 concepts across the three competency areas.

## Categories

### 1. Foundation Concepts (FOUND)
Core principles that underpin the rest of the course — the engineering design process, the scientific method, and how they relate to each other.

Concepts: Engineering Design Process, Scientific Method, Hypothesis Testing, Engineering vs Science

### 2. Career Exploration (CAREER)
Researching engineering fields, specializations, educational pathways, and the credentials that lead to engineering careers.

Concepts: Engineering Career Research, Engineering Specializations, Field of Interest Selection, Postsecondary Education Options, Engineering Programs, College Admissions Process, Degree Requirements, Professional Certifications, Licensure Requirements, Professional Engineer (PE) License, Engineer in Training (EIT) Exam

### 4. Employment Preparation (EMPLOY)
Practical tools and skills for entering the engineering workforce — portfolios, resumes, applications, and interviews.

Concepts: Work Portfolio, Portfolio Components, Resume Writing, Cover Letter Writing, Job Application Process, Engineering Internships, Interview Skills, Mock Interview Practice, Professional Readiness

### 5. Engineering Ethics (ETHICS)
The ethical frameworks and responsibilities engineers hold toward society, clients, and the profession.

Concepts: Engineering Ethics, Code of Ethics, Professional Responsibility, Public Safety in Engineering, Ethical Decision Making, Personal vs Professional Ethics, Conflict of Interest

### 6. Intellectual Property (IP)
Types of intellectual property protections, how they are enforced, and the consequences of infringement.

Concepts: Intellectual Property, Patents, Copyrights, Trademarks, Trade Secrets, IP Protection and Enforcement, IP Infringement

### 7. Design Process (DESIGN)
The iterative steps of engineering design from problem identification through prototype development and optimization.

Concepts: Problem Identification, Needs Assessment, Opportunity Recognition, Constraints and Criteria, Brainstorming Solutions, Prototype Development, Testing and Iteration, Design Optimization, Design Safety Evaluation, Safety Standards

### 8. Systems Thinking (SYST)
Understanding engineering problems as interconnected systems with inputs, outputs, feedback loops, and subsystems.

Concepts: Systems Thinking, System Inputs and Outputs, Feedback Loops, Subsystem Interactions

### 9. Proposals and Review (PROP)
Writing, structuring, defending, and peer-reviewing engineering project proposals.

Concepts: Proposal Writing, Project Proposal Structure, Engineering Challenge Definition, Proposal Defense, Peer Review Process, Proposal Review Criteria, Constructive Feedback

### 10. Project Management (PROJMGT)
Planning, scheduling, budgeting, and managing risk throughout the engineering practicum project.

Concepts: Project Management, Project Management Software, Work Breakdown Structure, Schedule Development, Scheduling Tools, Gantt Chart, Milestone Planning, Budget Research Methods, Cost Estimation, Cost-Benefit Analysis, Budget Development, Resource Allocation, Work Schedule Creation, Risk Identification, Risk Analysis, Risk Mitigation Strategies

### 11. Communication and Documentation (COMM)
Documenting project progress, modeling solutions, and communicating results to stakeholders and the public.

Concepts: Technical Report Writing, Documentation Standards, Report Structure, Modeling Techniques, Simulation Software, Stakeholder Identification, Stakeholder Communication, Presentation Skills, Visual Communication, Final Project Presentation
