## Engineering Practicum IV Honors

This learning graph is the foundational data structure for the Engineering Practicum IV Honors intelligent textbook. It maps 85 key concepts across three competency areas — Employability Skills, Intellectual Property, and Practicum Project Design — and defines the prerequisite relationships between them.

The learning graph supports multiple learning pathways and helps students understand how concepts build upon one another throughout the course.

## What is a Learning Graph?

A learning graph is a Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) in which:

- Each **node** represents a concept students must learn
- Each **arrow** represents a learning dependency — the concept at the tail should be learned before the concept at the head

## Concept Categories

The 85 concepts are organized into 11 categories:

- **Foundation Concepts** — Engineering design process, scientific method, and their relationship
- **Career Exploration** — Engineering fields, specializations, and postsecondary pathways
- **Credentials and Licensing** — Degrees, certifications, PE license, and EIT exam
- **Employment Preparation** — Portfolios, resumes, applications, and interviews
- **Engineering Ethics** — Professional codes, responsibility, and ethical decision making
- **Intellectual Property** — Patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, and enforcement
- **Design Process** — Problem identification through prototype development and optimization
- **Systems Thinking** — Inputs, outputs, feedback loops, and subsystem interactions
- **Proposals and Review** — Writing, structuring, defending, and peer reviewing proposals
- **Project Management** — Scheduling, budgeting, risk analysis, and project tools
- **Communication and Documentation** — Reports, modeling, stakeholder communication, and presentations
