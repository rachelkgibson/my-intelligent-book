Course Description: Engineering Practicum IV Honors

Course Overview

Engineering Practicum IV Honors enables students to examine technology and engineering fundamentals related to solving real-world problems. Students examine ethics and intellectual property and design a practicum project — a culmination of knowledge and skill gained in previous engineering courses. In addition, students continue to investigate engineering specialty fields and related careers to determine whether they are good candidates for postsecondary educational opportunities in engineering.

This course serves as the capstone experience of the high school engineering sequence, integrating technical, professional, and interpersonal skills developed throughout prior coursework. Students work independently and collaboratively to propose, design, execute, document, and present an original engineering solution to a real-world challenge.

Target Audience

High school students (Honors level)

Prerequisites

Engineering II with instructor permission, or Engineering III

Main Topics Covered

  1. Personal and Professional Qualities — creativity, critical thinking, initiative, integrity, and work ethic
  2. Interpersonal and Team Skills — conflict resolution, communication, diversity, customer service, and collaboration
  3. Professional Competencies — career management, information literacy, information security, current IT systems, mathematical applications, workplace safety, and professionalism
  4. Industry Examination — planning, management, financial responsibility, technical production, technology principles, labor issues, community issues, and health/safety/environmental concerns
  5. Student Organization and Civic Engagement — purposes of student organizations, leadership, membership responsibilities, and internet safety
  6. Work-Based Learning (WBL) — types of WBL, career exploration, and reflective practice
  7. Employability Skills — career research, educational pathways, portfolio development, internship/job application processes, engineering design vs. scientific method
  8. Intellectual Property and Ethics — engineering ethics, intellectual property rights, and professional vs. personal ethics
  9. Practicum Project Design and Execution — systems thinking, engineering design process, proposal development, peer review, project management, scheduling, budgeting, cost estimation, modeling and simulation, risk analysis, and stakeholder communication
  10. Career Preparation — postsecondary research, certifications and licensure, mock interviews, and technical life skills

Topics NOT Covered

  • Advanced college-level engineering mathematics (calculus, differential equations)
  • Discipline-specific engineering coursework (e.g., civil, electrical, mechanical in depth)
  • Manufacturing or fabrication at an industrial scale
  • Post-secondary coursework content (university-level engineering theory)

Learning Outcomes

After this course, students will be able to:

Remember

Retrieve, recognize, and recall relevant knowledge

  • Identify types of intellectual property rights and how they are protected and enforced
  • Identify the need or opportunity for an engineering solution
  • Identify educational requirements for a chosen engineering field
  • Identify the process of applying for an engineering internship or job
  • Identify the purposes and goals of a student organization
  • Identify the types of work-based learning (WBL) opportunities available
  • Identify Internet safety issues and procedures for complying with acceptable use standards
  • Research postsecondary education opportunities related to engineering
  • Research cost-estimation tools and methods used in the engineering field
  • Maintain a working knowledge of current information-technology (IT) systems

Understand

Construct meaning from instructional messages

  • Explain how ethical behavior of engineers is essential to the betterment of society
  • Explain the benefits and responsibilities of membership in student and professional/civic organizations
  • Demonstrate an understanding of information security
  • Demonstrate big-picture thinking within an engineering or organizational context
  • Examine principles of technology that underlie an industry or organization
  • Examine aspects of planning within an industry or organization
  • Examine aspects of financial responsibility within an industry or organization
  • Examine labor issues related to an industry or organization
  • Examine community issues related to an industry or organization
  • Examine health, safety, and environmental issues related to an industry or organization

Apply

Carry out or use procedures in given situations

  • Apply mathematical skills to job-specific engineering tasks
  • Apply model and simulation techniques to create a working model
  • Use the engineering design process to complete the practicum project
  • Use engineering project management tools throughout the practicum
  • Develop a schedule using industry-standard applications to ensure project completion
  • Develop a budget for the practicum project
  • Create a work schedule aligned with project milestones
  • Demonstrate proficiency with technologies, tools, and machines relevant to the chosen engineering field
  • Demonstrate workplace safety practices appropriate to the engineering environment
  • Participate in a work-based learning experience when appropriate

Analyze

Break material into parts and determine relationships

  • Analyze the similarities and differences between the engineering design process and the scientific method
  • Analyze the potential risk list for the practicum project
  • Examine technical and production skills required of workers within an industry
  • Examine aspects of management within an industry or organization
  • Compare requirements for select professional engineering certifications and licensure
  • Compare professional and personal ethics in the context of engineering practice
  • Demonstrate information-literacy skills by critically evaluating engineering and career resources
  • Reflect on lessons learned during the work-based learning experience
  • Explore career opportunities related to the WBL experience
  • Research a principal field of interest in engineering and its scope

Evaluate

Make judgments based on criteria and standards

  • Evaluate the safety of engineering designs against established standards
  • Peer review all project proposals using structured criteria
  • Demonstrate critical thinking and problem solving in evaluating engineering challenges
  • Demonstrate conflict-resolution skills when navigating team disagreements
  • Demonstrate customer service skills in communicating with project stakeholders
  • Demonstrate career- and life-management skills by assessing personal strengths against engineering pathways
  • Demonstrate continuous learning and adaptability when faced with project setbacks
  • Manage time and resources effectively throughout the practicum timeline
  • Demonstrate respect for diversity in team and professional settings
  • Demonstrate integrity and professionalism in all project and workplace interactions

Create

Put elements together to form coherent wholes; includes capstone projects

  • Design and complete an original engineering practicum project using the full engineering design process
  • Use systems thinking to develop a comprehensive engineering solution
  • Present a proposal to an engineering challenge, incorporating research, design, and feasibility analysis
  • Document the full progression of the engineering practicum in a formal technical report
  • Communicate the final solution to stakeholders through a professional presentation
  • Build a complete work portfolio demonstrating skills and accomplishments across the engineering sequence
  • Demonstrate leadership skills through participation in student organization activities such as meetings, programs, and projects
  • Demonstrate creativity and innovation in developing novel engineering solutions
  • Demonstrate initiative and self-direction throughout the independent practicum project
  • Exhibit technical and life skills necessary for success in the engineering field
